Amal Alamuddin

When we take a look at George Clooney’s past partners, we see a bevy of tall, tanned blonde women that don’t necessarily have an accolades under their belt. And as an A-list star, why would you need to stray away from the constant posse of Victoria’s Secret supermodels that are readily available to you?

Because a certain human rights lawyer and goddess named Amal Alamuddin graced him with his presence, and his world was never the same after that. Neither was ours.

The Lebanese-British activist and lawyer grew up in England, studying at the prestigious institutes Oxford University and NYU before embarking on her journey as a lawyer.

by Naima Karp
12/12, 9999

A lamuddin has worked on the Enron case, along with Kofi Annan, for the United Nations commission on Syria. She’s defended Wiki-Leaks editor-in-chief Julian Assange and former Ukrainian prime minster Yulia Tymoshenko, but has always remembered to take the side of the underdog and represent them too.

Much of her work also revolves around the mistreatment of women; Amal has worked closely with abused Yazidi women.

If you were set to marry George Clooney, you would think that your lifestyle would change drastically. But that’s one thing that Amal ensured never happened. Her lifestyle was dedicating all of her time to helping others in need through her fight for justice, and she refused to sacrifice that just because she was marrying a celebrity.

F Fortunately, Amal married not just a celebrity but a socially and politically minded man who supports and contributes to her activism and philanthropic efforts.

While Amal has made incredible strides with her humanitarian and poverty aid work, the media still focuses on her style over her impressive work. As she fought against a Turkish politician denying the genocide of over 1.5 million people, journalists were still more interested in what designer threads the lawyer would tout.

She jokingly responded: “Ede and Ravenscroft,” to them, the brand who has been making legal robes for centuries. This cheeky comment diverted a superficial question, showing Amal take control of the conversation to give the voiceless a voice, inspiring many in the process.

G eorge must have recognized the substance underneath Amal's jaw-dropping, effortless beauty, realizing that he needed to hold on to this other-worldly woman. She handled the transition to fame in the public eye flawlessly, but still remained focused and dedicated to her craft.

Her goal isn’t to be photographed by the paparazzi —— it’s to create world peace and end suffering. She uses her clout and smarts to fight for the rights of others, rather than her name and appearance. She’s a reminder for us to stop obsessing over editing Instagram photos, and instead do something to better someone else’s life.

Amal is proof that you can be a stunning humanitarian, and not let celebrity overshadow that fact. While many celebrities use existing fame to aid their humanitarian efforts, Amal is just doing her job, dedicating herself selflessly to the plight of others as she has since Day One.

The Woman
“BELIEVE IN YOURSELF”

Oprah Winfrey has always been about making women feel good about themselves--i.e. inspiring them to “live their best lives.”

Oprah is much more than a TV personality and her recent speech at the Golden Globes (where she received the prestigious Cecil B. DeMille Award) was one of her most “golden” moments yet. Immediately following her now-legendary early January acceptance speech,

the media and fans were suggesting that she run for president in the next election!

While there are many people in the US with rags to riches tales, Oprah’s “back story” is special. She was a minority female who overcame poverty and childhood molestation to become a beauty queen and then a local TV personality in Baltimore circa 1976 with the show People Are Talking.

by Anne Raso
12/12, 9999

S he parlayed her smarts and natural charisma into a national self-named talk show in 1986 and then went on to make the careers of others like Dr. Oz and Dr. Phil by giving them guest spots. As we all now know, Oprah then parlayed her successful talk show into a vast empire that includes OWN TV, an acting career that is presently on hold, a national magazine and a huge network of charities that includes building schools in poor areas around the world. (Oprah appears on the The 50 Most Generous Americans list every year.)

In 2003, she became the first African-American billionaire in the world. When a consumer product of any sort gets mentioned as one of “Oprah’s Favorite Things” in an article or online post, it immediately sells out. Few people wield this kind of power who are as utterly likable as Oprah—she still seems like “one of us” despite her power and wealth and she is arguably the single most influential female in the world today.

Oprah’s belief system is based on the Golden Rule and people who have met her or have worked with her say that she makes you feel important and takes what you say seriously. In one of her Lifeclass programs, Lady O summed the key to a happy life up in one sentence: “You must believe in yourself and believe that the life you want is well within your reach.”

Oprah is so beloved because she is not only an impeccable speaker but because she lays her soul bare while most celebrities are guarded. Winfrey’s beliefs on suffering, gratitude, generosity, forgiveness and other spiritual topics are ones that stick with us. She tells women to never forget where they came from, love themselves the way they are, to offer help to others and to always put aside time for pampering even if they are not affluent. Oprah is about “ah ha” moments and being spiritual even if it is not via organized religion.

As we go to press, the rumors are still flying that Oprah is considering running for president even though “the queen of all media” has not commented on this firsthand; back in October, Oprah dismissed that she was considering running for any sort of political office. Whether she runs for the 2020 US presidency or not, she will always be a leader whose heart of gold leads the way for inspiration and positivity in women’s lives.
